MagnaDesk

A digital corkboard that lives inside your Windows wallpaper. Sticky notes, images, drawings, and live widgets — baked right into your desktop. One keystroke (Ctrl+Alt+Q) drops anything on your clipboard straight onto the canvas.

What Is MagnaDesk?

MagnaDesk places interactive sticky notes, images, drawings, and live widgets directly into your Windows wallpaper. Items are rendered into the wallpaper bitmap itself — there's no visible overlay window in normal use. They sit below your desktop icons, behave like part of the wallpaper, and use zero system resources when you're not editing.

When you need to add or edit items, press Ctrl+Shift+M to open Edit Mode — a transparent overlay spans all your monitors with a full editing toolbar. Make your changes, press Esc, and everything bakes back into the wallpaper.

Three Simple States

Viewing Mode

Your items are baked into the wallpaper. No overlay window, no running process drawing on your screen. Items sit below desktop icons like they're part of the wallpaper — because they are. Zero resource overhead.

Edit Mode

Press Ctrl+Shift+M or click the tray icon. A transparent overlay appears across all monitors with an editing toolbar. Add, move, resize, rotate, and delete items. Press Esc to bake everything back into the wallpaper.

Hidden

Tuck everything away with a single toggle — just your wallpaper, no canvas. Quick Paste (Ctrl+Alt+Q) still works while hidden, so you can keep capturing things even with the canvas out of sight. Toggle back when you want it all again.

Headline Feature

Quick Paste — Ctrl+Alt+Q

This is the fastest way to get anything off your screen and onto your desktop on Windows. From any app, press Ctrl+Alt+Q and MagnaDesk reads your clipboard and drops the contents straight onto the canvas — no Edit Mode, no toolbar, no extra clicks.

  • Clipboard has an image? It's placed at a random spot on the canvas and baked into the wallpaper.
  • Clipboard has text? A new sticky note is created with that text — still fully editable, never flattened into a bitmap.
  • Canvas hidden? Quick Paste still works. Items keep accumulating in the background, ready for whenever you toggle the canvas back on.

Pair it with Win+Shift+S for the fastest snip-to-desktop loop on Windows: snip anything on screen, hit Ctrl+Alt+Q, done. Reference screenshots, paragraphs of text you want to come back to, code snippets, quotes — all one keystroke from your wallpaper.

Headline Feature

Quick Note Edit — Double-Tap Ctrl+Alt+Q

Same shortcut, two gestures: one tap pastes, two taps edits. Double-tap Ctrl+Alt+Q within 400 ms and a small floating note editor pops up centered on screen — no Edit Mode, no overlay across all your monitors. It's the quickest way to jot down a thought or fix a typo on a note without stopping what you're doing.

  • Floating, always-on-top editor — a compact dark window showing one sticky note at a time, right where you're working.
  • Cycle through every note — the ❮ / ❯ arrows step through all the notes on your canvas, and the header shows the count so you know where you are.
  • Edit, resize, recolor — type straight into the note, nudge the font size with − / +, or recolor it with the five color swatches.
  • + New note — drops a fresh blank note on the canvas (random spot, tilt, and color, just like Quick Paste) and jumps the editor right to it with the caret ready.
  • 🗑 Delete note — removes the note you're looking at after a quick confirm (there's no undo). Delete the last one and the window simply closes.
  • Esc closes it — and only the notes you actually changed trigger a wallpaper re-bake. Cycle through everything without editing and your wallpaper is left exactly as it was.

Scroll inside a long note and the wallpaper bakes the lines you were actually viewing — not always the first line at the top.

What You Can Put on Your Desktop

Sticky Notes

Editable text notes in five colors — yellow, pink, mint, blue, and white. Move, resize, and rotate them anywhere on your desktop.

Images

Place any JPG or PNG on your desktop with crop support. Or use Win+Shift+S to snip anything on screen, then Ctrl+V to paste it — web pages, spreadsheets, emails, anything visible.

Drawing & Shapes

Draw directly on your desktop with the pen tool. Five colors, adjustable stroke width (1–12px), and a canvas size slider so you can write large and shrink the result. Eraser tool removes individual strokes. Plus six shape tools for when freehand isn't precise enough.

Shape Tools

Beyond freehand ink, the drawing canvas now draws clean shapes — and every shape is stored as an ordinary stroke, so it bakes into the wallpaper and undoes just like anything else you draw.

  • Six shapes — Line, Arrow (with barbed tip), Circle, Rectangle, Triangle, and a 5-point Star, each drawn with a single drag.
  • Shift+drag to snap a line — hold Shift while drawing freehand and the stroke becomes a clean straight line between where you started and stopped.
  • Right-click for the tool menu — right-click an active drawing canvas to switch between Freehand and any shape, clear all strokes, or finish up. The current tool is checkmarked.
  • Rotate while you place — sizing a Rectangle or Triangle, hold Shift or Spacebar to lock the size and rotate it around the pick point, then release to keep sizing.
  • Clean undo — Ctrl+Z removes a whole arrow (shaft and both barbs) in one step, and even "Clear All Strokes" can be undone.

Live Widgets

Widgets display live data — from your Microsoft account and from the world around you — rendered right into your wallpaper. Connect multiple accounts and each account-based widget can pull from a specific account or aggregate across all of them.

Google account widgets are temporarily switched off while I move the app to production — Google Tasks, Calendar, and Gmail support is coming soon.

Task List

Display tasks from a single Microsoft To Do list (Google Tasks coming soon). Checkboxes, due dates, and completion status — all visible at a glance on your desktop.

To-Do List

Aggregates tasks across all your lists, grouped by due date — Overdue, Today, Tomorrow, Upcoming, and No Date. Like the To Do app's smart view, but on your wallpaper.

Calendar

Upcoming events grouped by day with subject, time, and location. Shows 7 days ahead by default. Works with Outlook (Google Calendar coming soon).

OneNote

Your most recently modified OneNote pages with notebook, section, and relative timestamps. Quick visual of what you've been working on.

Mail

Unread messages from your inbox with sender, subject, and preview. Red indicator for high-importance messages. Shows "Inbox zero" when you're caught up.

Radar

Live weather radar baked into your wallpaper — worldwide, no account needed. Search any city to drop the view where you live. Four zoom levels (Wide ~500 km, Regional ~250 km, Local ~125 km, Town ~60 km) pull tighter areas at full source resolution, so closer weather shows real detail instead of bigger pixels. Pan the view north/south/east/west to center on your town, or hit ⌖ to recenter. Zoomed views sit over an Esri World Street Map basemap so storms line up with real towns and coastlines.

Weather

A worldwide forecast on your wallpaper — no account, no API key. Search any city and the widget pulls current conditions and the days ahead from Open-Meteo. It shares the same city search as the Radar widget, so picking your location once is enough.

Widget Features

  • Connect multiple Microsoft accounts simultaneously (Google accounts coming soon)
  • Each widget can show data from one account or all accounts combined
  • 11 background color themes — Dark, Slate, Navy, Cobalt, Teal, Forest, Olive, Rust, Wine, Plum, Charcoal
  • Resizing reveals more or fewer items — text stays readable, never scales
  • Auto-refresh on a configurable interval, plus manual refresh

Built-In Wallpaper Engine

MagnaDesk doesn't just draw on your wallpaper — it manages it too.

To render the canvas the way I wanted — baked right into the desktop, below the icons, with no overlay window in the way — I had to take over the wallpaper itself. Once I was already managing your wallpaper, it felt wrong to do it halfway. So I'm building it out into a proper wallpaper engine: single images, slideshows, solid colors, web sources, and more to come. If MagnaDesk is going to own your wallpaper, I want it to be the best wallpaper experience on your machine.

Single Image

Set any image file as your wallpaper with your choice of fit — Stretch, Fill, Fit (letterbox), Center, or Tile.

Slideshow

Cycle through images in a folder at a configurable interval. Sequential or shuffle. Advance anytime with Ctrl+Alt+W or from the right-click desktop menu.

Solid Color

Pick a preset color or enter a hex value for a clean, minimal background.

Web Image Sources

Search and download wallpapers from Unsplash, Pexels, and Wallhaven directly in the app. Uses your own free API keys — they never leave your machine.

Editing Tools

The editing toolbar floats at the bottom of your screen. Drag it anywhere with the grip handle.

  • Select (S) — click to select items. Drag to move. Corner handles to resize, rotation handle to rotate, × to delete
  • Add Note (N) — creates a new sticky note at the center of your primary monitor
  • Add Image (I) — opens a file picker to place an image on the canvas
  • Paste (Ctrl+V) — pastes a clipboard image. Use Win+Shift+S to capture anything on screen first
  • Pen (P) — freehand drawing with adjustable stroke width and canvas size, plus six shape tools (Line, Arrow, Circle, Rectangle, Triangle, Star) from the canvas right-click menu
  • Eraser (E) — click a stroke to erase it
  • Undo (Ctrl+Z) — reverses the last action
  • Done (Esc) — exits Edit Mode and bakes everything into the wallpaper

Multi-Monitor Support

The MagnaDesk canvas spans all connected monitors. Place items on any screen and they'll be baked into the correct position in the wallpaper.

  • Items on disconnected monitors automatically migrate to the nearest active monitor
  • When the monitor reconnects, items return to their original positions
  • Use "Recover Off-Screen Items" from the tray menu if anything gets lost

Account Integration

Microsoft

Sign in using the device code flow — you'll get a code to enter at microsoft.com/devicelogin. Grants access to To Do, Calendar, OneNote, and Mail. Works with both personal and work/school accounts.

Google — coming soon

Sign in via browser-based OAuth for access to Google Calendar, Gmail, and Google Tasks, with multiple accounts supported. Google connectivity is temporarily switched off while I finish moving the app to production — it's coming soon.

All tokens are encrypted via Windows DPAPI and stored locally. Nothing leaves your machine.
