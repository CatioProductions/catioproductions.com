My Rescue Mission

I'm actively TNRing the stray colonies in my own neighborhood. Every app sale funds that work — and helps me reach the next colony.

What Is TNR?

Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) is the humane, effective approach to managing community cat populations. Cats are humanely trapped, spayed or neutered by a veterinarian, and returned to their outdoor home. Over time, this stabilizes and gradually reduces colony sizes.

How I Help

Local Colonies First

There are 4 stray colonies within a half-mile of my home — all being fed by neighbors, none being TNR'd. I'm working through them myself: trapping, transporting to the clinic, and returning the cats to their caregivers.

Funded by App Sales

Proceeds from every app I sell go straight into this work — clinic costs, traps, food, shelters, and medical supplies for the cats and the residents already caring for them.

Beyond My Block

Once my local colonies are stable, I'll keep working with my neighbors to maintain them — and start sending supplies and funds to other rescuers TNRing in nearby communities.
